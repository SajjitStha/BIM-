Computer Ethics Commandments

Commandment 1:

Thou shalt respect others' privacy and confidentiality.

Always be mindful of the personal information and data of others. Do not invade their privacy or use their data without their permission.

Commandment 2:

Thou shalt not engage in cyberbullying or harassment.

Respectful and constructive communication is important. Avoid any form of online harassment that can cause harm to others.

Commandment 3:

Thou shalt use software and media ethically.

Respect software licenses and copyrights. Avoid pirated software and illegal downloads. Use media responsibly and within legal limits.

Commandment 4:

Thou shalt protect thy computer and networks.

Do not engage in hacking or unauthorized access to systems. Maintain up-to-date security measures to protect your computer and networks from malware and cyber threats.

Commandment 5:

Thou shalt be honest and ethical in online interactions.

Do not engage in deceptive practices such as spreading false information, engaging in phishing, or impersonating others online.
